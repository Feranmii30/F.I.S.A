Neapolitan Margherita Pizza

Chef Feranmi

Trattoria Napoletana, Rome

4.9 (328 reviews)
30 min
320 cal

Description

The Neapolitan Margherita pizza is the quintessential Italian classic, featuring a thin, soft, and chewy crust with a slightly charred exterior from the intense heat of a wood-fired oven. Topped with sweet San Marzano tomatoes, creamy buffalo mozzarella, fresh basil, and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, this pizza represents the colors of the Italian flag and the pure flavors of Naples.

Ingredients

  • 250g Tipo 00 flour
  • 150ml lukewarm water
  • 5g fresh yeast or 3g dry yeast
  • 5g sea salt
  • 150g San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 125g buffalo mozzarella, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Neapolitan Margherita Pizza

4.9 (328 reviews)
Chef Feranmi
PREP TIME

20 minutes

COOK TIME

10 minutes

SERVINGS

2 servings

DIFFICULTY

Intermediate

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 250g Tipo 00 flour
  • 150ml lukewarm water
  • 5g fresh yeast or 3g dry yeast
  • 5g sea salt
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

For the Topping

  • 150g San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 125g buffalo mozzarella, torn into pieces
  • Fresh basil leaves (8-10 leaves)
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt, to taste

Instructions

1

Prepare the Dough

In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. In a separate small bowl, dissolve the yeast in lukewarm water and let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy. Make a well in the center of the flour and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms.

2

Knead the Dough

Transfer the dough to a floured surface and knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Form into a ball and place in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise in a warm place for at least 2 hours, or until doubled in size.

3

Preheat Your Oven

Preheat your oven to the highest temperature (ideally 500°F/260°C or higher) with a pizza stone or heavy baking sheet inside for at least 30 minutes.

4

Shape the Pizza

Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions (for two 10-inch pizzas). On a floured surface, use your hands to gently stretch one portion of dough into a 10-inch circle, creating a slightly thicker rim around the edge.

5

Add Toppings

Transfer the shaped dough to a piece of parchment paper or a floured pizza peel. Spread half of the crushed tomatoes over the dough, leaving a small border around the edge. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt. Arrange half of the mozzarella pieces evenly over the tomatoes.

6

Bake the Pizza

Carefully slide the pizza (with the parchment paper if using) onto the preheated stone or baking sheet. Bake for 8-10 minutes, or until the crust is puffed and charred in spots and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

7

Finish and Serve

Remove the pizza from the oven and immediately drizzle with olive oil. Scatter fresh basil leaves on top. Slice and serve immediately while hot. Repeat with the remaining dough and toppings for the second pizza.

Chef's Notes & Tips

Dough Preparation

For the best results, prepare the dough a day in advance and let it slow-rise in the refrigerator overnight. This develops more complex flavors and improves the texture. Just remove it from the refrigerator about 2 hours before you plan to use it to allow it to come to room temperature.

Ingredient Selection

The quality of ingredients makes a huge difference in this simple pizza. Use the best quality San Marzano tomatoes you can find, and fresh buffalo mozzarella (or fior di latte) rather than pre-shredded cheese. For the most authentic flavor, use Italian Tipo 00 flour, which has a finer grind than all-purpose flour.

Cooking Method

Traditional Neapolitan pizza is cooked in a wood-fired oven at temperatures around 900°F (480°C) for just 60-90 seconds. To approximate this at home, use the highest temperature your oven can reach and a pizza stone or steel that's been preheated for at least 45 minutes. Some home cooks have success using their oven's broiler setting for the last minute of cooking to char the crust.

Serving Suggestions

Neapolitan pizza is traditionally eaten with a knife and fork, not folded or picked up by the slice. Serve immediately after cooking for the best texture and flavor. Pair with a simple arugula salad dressed with lemon and olive oil, and a glass of light Italian red wine like Chianti or Montepulciano d'Abruzzo.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overworking the dough, which makes it tough
  • Using too many toppings, which weighs down the crust
  • Adding fresh basil before baking (it will burn)
  • Not preheating your oven and stone long enough
